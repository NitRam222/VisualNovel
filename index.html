Ten Gospel encounters · One unfolding invitation

Ten Gospel encounters

Encounters
with Jesus

Choose a story. Scroll slowly. Listen closely. Each journey begins with people who think they know what is happening, until Christ reveals that He has been nearer than they imagined.

Choose a journey ↓
01Luke 24:13–35

Stay With Us

Two disciples leave Jerusalem carrying grief. A stranger joins their road and quietly turns despair into recognition.

02Mark 4:35–41

Peace, Be Still

A boat disappears into violent water. The disciples find Jesus asleep while everything around them says they are about to die.

03John 20:11–18

Called by Name

Mary remains beside an empty tomb. Through tears she mistakes the risen Christ for a gardener, until He says one word.

04John 21:1–19

Do You Love Me?

Peter returns to the sea carrying the memory of his denial. On the shore, Jesus prepares breakfast and restores him beside another charcoal fire.

05John 11:17–44

Come Out

Four days after Lazarus is buried, Jesus enters a village filled with grief, weeps beside the tomb, and calls into the darkness.

06John 4:4–30, 39–42

Living Water

A woman comes to the well when no one else will be there. Jesus meets her in the heat, tells the truth without contempt, and gives her a new story to carry home.

07Mark 5:21–43

Talitha Koum

A father begs Jesus to hurry. The journey is interrupted, the message arrives too late, and Christ walks into a room where everyone has already surrendered to death.

08Mark 5:25–34

Take Heart, Daughter

After twelve years of hidden suffering, a woman reaches through a crowd for the edge of Jesus’ garment. He refuses to let her leave healed but unseen.

09Mark 10:46–52

Let Me See

A blind beggar hears that Jesus is passing by. The crowd commands silence, but mercy stops for the voice everyone else has learned to ignore.

10Luke 23:32–43

Remember Me

At the end of a ruined life, a condemned man turns toward the crucified Jesus. His last request receives a promise larger than death.

Jesus

Woman

Jesus

Narrator

not rejected.

Chapter IVLiving Water
Woman

Jesus

Chapter VWhat Will You Leave?

Chapter VIThe Water Jar
Narrator

Woman

to hide

Epilogue

Known completely.
Loved still.

Jesus did not save her by ignoring the truth. He spoke the truth in a love strong enough to free her from hiding. The well became the place where shame lost its name.

“Come and see a man who told me everything I have ever done.”John 4:29
